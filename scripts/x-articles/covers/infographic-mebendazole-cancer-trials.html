Mebendazole in Oncology: What Human Trials Show

Interesting preclinical science, but human data remain small and inconclusive. Poor oral bioavailability is the recurring barrier.

NCT02644291

Recurrent pediatric brain tumors

CompletedPhase 1n=16

Higher-dose monotherapy tolerated short-term; meaningful responses rare.

NCT03628079

Advanced GI cancers

TerminatedPhase 1/2n=11

Stopped early for lack of clear efficacy after modest enrollment.

Central barrier: bioavailability

Even at high oral doses, many patients fail to reach plasma levels associated with anticancer activity in preclinical models.

Bottom line

Not FDA-approved for cancer. Promising lab data, inconclusive human trials. Improved formulations may be needed before larger efficacy studies can fairly test the hypothesis.
