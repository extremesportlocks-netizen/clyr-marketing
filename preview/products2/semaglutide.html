The Details

What you are actually getting.

A single-receptor GLP-1

Semaglutide acts on the GLP-1 receptor and is compounded with vitamin B12, which is commonly added to support energy during treatment.

Dose titration

Your provider starts you low and increases gradually to limit side effects. Compounded steps typically move through the following on your provider's schedule:

  • Starting and early doses, generally in the 0.2 mg to 0.9 mg range
  • Higher maintenance doses, generally in the 1.5 mg to 2.2 mg range
  • Your provider sets the exact dose and pace; this is not self-directed

Important Safety Information

Semaglutide is a prescription medication provided only after review by a licensed provider; not everyone qualifies. It should not be used by individuals with a personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma or multiple endocrine neoplasia syndrome type 2, and carries risks including pancreatitis, gallbladder problems, and gastrointestinal effects. Not for use in pregnancy. This product is compounded; compounded medications are not FDA-approved and are not reviewed by the FDA for safety, efficacy, or quality before reaching patients.
