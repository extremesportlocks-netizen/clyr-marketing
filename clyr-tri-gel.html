Frequently Asked

Questions about
CLYR Tri Gel?

A compounded prescription gel combining clindamycin, benzoyl peroxide, and adapalene. It follows the same triple-therapy concept as branded Cabtreo, prepared by a licensed compounding pharmacy.

Cabtreo is an FDA-approved branded product. CLYR Tri Gel is a compounded prescription with the same three active ingredient classes, prepared individually for each patient. Your provider will discuss which option fits your case.

Typically a pea-sized amount to clean, dry skin once nightly on affected areas. Your provider will confirm instructions and whether to buffer with moisturizer.

Retinoids can cause dryness, peeling, or mild irritation for the first 2–4 weeks. Most patients improve as skin acclimates. Contact your provider if irritation is severe.

Tell your provider about all topicals and oral medications. Combining multiple strong actives without guidance can irritate skin.

Not for use during pregnancy or breastfeeding. Avoid if you have a known allergy to clindamycin, benzoyl peroxide, or adapalene. Your provider reviews full history before prescribing.

Important Safety Information

CLYR Tri Gel is a compounded prescription topical. Common side effects include dryness, peeling, redness, and irritation. Benzoyl peroxide may bleach fabrics. Adapalene increases sun sensitivity; use sunscreen daily. Clindamycin topical products have been associated with colitis in rare cases; stop use and contact your provider if you develop severe diarrhea. Compounded medications have not been reviewed by the FDA for safety or effectiveness. Individual results may vary.
